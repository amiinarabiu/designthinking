Our Team

Introducing our team members.

The Board

Zainab Mohammad (MBBS)

Zainab is a trained physician who is dedicated to the humanitarian cause. She is currently a clinical lead and program officer at Reconnect Health Development Initiative (RDHI), where she leads initiatives for mental health and drug abuse awareness. She facilitates capacity building and psychological first aid training and coordinates safe civic space and support groups formation. She has previously worked as a medical officer at Abuja Clinics, Lagos University Teaching Hospital, and National Hospital Abuja. She works closely with government and non-governmental institutions on mental health and drug abuse issues. She enjoys TV shows, travelling, and culinary experiences in her spare time. Zainab obtained her MBBS degree from the Gulf Medical University Ajman, UAE. She sits on the DTiD Board.

Jean Ogechi Igwegbe (BSc, MSc)

Jean is a public health practitioner, a writer, and a mental health advocate with years of experience in the community development sector. She designs and implements sustainable prevention and treatment interventions for people affected by mental health and substance abuse problems, focusing on young people. Jean, a Mandela Young Africa Leaders Initiative (YALI) fellow, holds a BSc in Biochemistry from the University of Birmingham, UK, and a Master’s in Public Health from the University of the West of England, Bristol, UK. She also has a certification from the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime in drug prevention, treatment, and care. She sits on the DTiD Board.

Fatima Muhammad (MMBS, PMP)

Fatima is a seasoned clinician and public health practitioner. She is currently an Analyst and Service Delivery Lead Smiles for Mothers Program for Niger State at Clinton Health Access Initiative. She advocates for gender mainstreaming in public health and has experience providing clinical care and psychological support to military officers and their families. She enjoys reading and saving the lives of women and children. She trained as a medical doctor at Elrazi University in Sudan. She has also completed the McKinsey and Company’s Forward Africa Program, Health Systems Strengthening Programs by John Hopkins School of Medicine and Project Management Professional certification by Google. She sits on the DTiD Board.

Ibraheem Abioye (MD, MPH, SM, Ph.D.)

Abioye is an accomplished physician and population health scientist. He is a Medical Director at Genentech, supporting the development of innovative medicines for rare diseases. He practiced general medicine for several years at rural and urban health facilities in Nigeria. He has extensive experience supporting the design, field coordination, and analysis of clinical trials and population studies in Nigeria, Tanzania, and The Philippines, with about three dozen publications in high-impact journals. He also coordinates Stats_Clinic, a nonprofit that delivers free web-based courses in epidemiology and statistics to healthcare professionals across African countries. Before working in biopharma, Abioye trained as a medical doctor at Lagos State University in Nigeria. He has two masters' degrees in public health and biostatistics and a Ph.D. in Population Health Science from Harvard University, USA. He sits on the DTiD Board.

Management

Muhammad Alhaji (BSc, MPh, Ph.D.)

Alhaji is a management consultant with over a decade of experience in the development sector. He has worked with the US-CDC-funded Public Health Information Surveillance, Solutions and Systems (PHIS3) Project as its pioneer Director of Data Science; Centre for International Health, Education and Biosecurity, University of Maryland, Baltimore as their Biostatistics Lead; and Solina Group as their Knowledge Management, Research and Training Lead. He is an Associate Fellow of the Royal Commonwealth Society, UK. Alhaji holds a BSc in Human Anatomy from Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria, MPH and Ph.D. from Brunei Darussalam, Brunei. He has received certificates in Leading Change from the University of Cambridge, UK, and Global Health Policy from the University of Brunei Darussalam. He founded the Group, sits on the DTiD Board, and currently serves as the Group Managing Partner.
